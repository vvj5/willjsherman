Book Mediation

This was a freelance contract that I worked on from start to finish, from initiation to production release.

I worked with a data programmer who assisted with the calendar API interface and some of the site logic.

I worked with the customer directly on the site needs and advised on information architecture, UX design and site branding.

I began the process with a lot of questions and branstorming, followed by multiple iterations of site wireframes, followed by more questions and more wireframes before really getting into the build.

I had to manage hours, functionality and progress to ensure that the project ran on time and on budget.

We used Trello collaboratively as a group to track the progress of the app development and to add, change and rank urgency of tasks and bugs.

I used pencil and paper for wireframing and draft iterations, Ruby on Rails, Bootstrap, HTML and CSS (and a little JavaScript) for the build.

A Complicated Layout

The aim of this project was to create, from scratch, UX and UI for a customer with a very, VERY complex website involving multiple layers of filtering and dashboard presentation.

I asked a lot of questions. A LOT of questions. Then I involved the customer in the drawing of site maps and navigation flows, which gave me what I needed to begin wireframing and prototyping.

I also worked remotely with a front-end developer, who I collaborated with extensively on some design and information architecture decisions, based on what he needed to input his dashboard data and what would help him best structure the site.

Unfortunately, I was not able (for privacy reasons) to talk directly to any of the client's customers, so I am currently in the iterative process of making adjustments to the site based on data being collected by Google Analytics.

The customers were remote, so meetings were held via GoToMeeting, where I would walk the customers through draft iterations, take feedback and suggest solutions.

I used Illustrator and InVision for wireframing and draft iterations, Materialize (a CSS shell), HTML and CSS for the build.

The Arcturus Connection

This was a site redesign for a very text-heavy client site.

An oddity of this project was that I had to actually edit a great deal of the copy (the client is not a native English speaker).

My process involved the interesting challenge of interacting with a very eccentric client who has very eccentric customers, and I ended-up doing most of my research by visiting probably a dozen high-ranked similar sites to get an idea of what kind of format the likely visitors would be used-to (user interviews were not possible, and I received no responses to the questionnaire I sent to his mailing list, which may reflect an error on my part, but I am honestly still not sure).

I then created my initial set of wireframes and site flow maps, which had to be completely scrapped due to some communication misunderstandings. My second set were similarly scrapped entirely, but my third set finally wowed the client, and we were able to move forward with info architecture, editing and adding content.

My KPI on this site is pretty simple- email enquiries. I also check SE keywords and analyze visitor click-through flows and dwell times.

I used Illustrator for wireframing and InVision for prototyping and draft iterations, Jekyll (I used Jekyll because the site is pretty blog-heavy), Materialize (a CSS shell), HTML and CSS for the build.
